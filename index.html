Fear vs. Fundamental Framework
When a stock hits your alert threshold, run through this before deploying capital. 6+ checks = strong fear-driven signal. Below 4 = likely fundamental — don't buy.
1
Is the core business model still intact?
Earnings/revenue trend still positive?
2
Is the drop macro/sector-wide, not company-specific?
Whole sector sold off, not just this name?
3
Are earnings/revenue still growing?
Is the most recent quarter still showing growth?
4
Is the competitive moat unchanged?
Pricing power, switching costs, or network effects intact?
5
Any insider buying into the dip?
Check SEC filings for Form 4s in last 30 days.
6
No new governance or legal issues?
No new DOJ, SEC, accounting, or auditor issues?
7
Does my original thesis still hold?
The reason you'd own this stock — is it unchanged?
⚡ Speed-Based Dip Signal Guide
The faster the drop, the more likely it's fear-driven. A slow bleed over months = institutional exit, not a dip.
🟢
High Conviction — 1–10 trading days
Sharp drop on identifiable catalyst. Volume spike 2–3x normal. Buy zone — run checklist and act.
🟡
Caution — 2–6 weeks
Could be fear, could be repricing. Wait for stabilization. Run full checklist. Don't chase.
🔴
Likely Not a Dip — 2+ months
Slow bleeds = institutional exit. Market has had time to digest. Avoid — this is how SMCI looked before collapse.
